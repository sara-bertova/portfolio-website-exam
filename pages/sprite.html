04.01.01 Sprite animation on a background

Focus?

The purpose of this assignment was to come up with our own character and background (sketch them and draw them in Illustrator), make a sprite sheet in Illustrator and eventually create a sprite animation on the background in CSS.

My role?

I chose Adventure Time as the source of inspiration for my character. Since I struggled to come up with an original character and just keep copying the ones from Adventure Time I decided to base the hair and clothing style on a specific person – Harry Styles. Besides the turn-around of my character, I also sketched my character’s face in different moods.

I decided that my animation will be the change of expression (bursting into laughter) rather than a walk cycle. I sketched that and draw it in Illustrator and do the same with the background. After creating a sprite sheet and finishing my sprite animation I filled out the rest of the worksheet about my character’s physical, psychological and sociological characteristics. I was also documenting the character development process along the way (through scans and screenshots).

New knowledge, perhaps?

First of all, I was introduced to the basic CSS transitions and animations. I’ve got better at paper sketching (even though I suffered a lot) and improved my drawing skills in Illustrator. Moreover, I’ve gained a new knowledge regarding 12 principles of animation, character and background design, and expression and walk cycle animation. I’ve managed to make a sprite and test it with an SWF-file. And finally, I’ve learnt how to get my sprite to move and place it on a background using CSS.
